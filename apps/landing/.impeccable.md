## Design Context

Synthesized from `docs/superpowers/specs/2026-04-20-landing-design.md`, `CONTENT.md`, `src/styles/tokens.css`, and deployed state at `rntme.com` (2026-04-20). Source of truth for any future `impeccable:*` skill invocations scoped to this app.

### Users

Tech leads and CTOs at AI-native product teams (2–10 engineers) who use Cursor/Claude Code to build internal workflow services — ticketing, approvals, ops consoles, back-office dashboards. They've shipped a first AI-generated service in a weekend and are now staring at service #5 with a migration they don't trust and events that fire twice on retry. They read landing pages the way senior engineers read landing pages: skeptical, scanning for architecture, allergic to hype.

Secondary audience: delivery leads at agencies shipping many similar workflow services per client.

### Brand Personality

Direct, engineer-first, editorial. Three-word personality: **terse, load-bearing, unimpressed**. The product voice is a senior architect explaining something honestly rather than selling it.

Voice rules already established:
- Room for exactly one playful line ("Make no mistakes") as §06 heading
- No hype, no emojis, no "power" / "effortless" / "seamless"
- No mention of SQLite, Turso, SQL, or any specific DB (triggers defensive arguments)
- Honest about maturity: "runtime core is stable; the control plane is in beta"

### Aesthetic Direction

**Editorial–technical**, not startup–SaaS. The vibe reference is a typeset monograph or an architecture journal laid over a terminal, not a Lovable/Vercel/Linear template.

Locked choices (do not drift):

- **Theme:** Light only. `--color-bg: oklch(99.0% 0.003 265)` — a cool near-white tinted toward brand indigo. Rationale: ICP reads the page in daylight on a work machine and needs to trust the page the way they trust documentation.
- **Palette:** OKLCH, brand hue 265 (indigo) for neutrals/focus, accent hue 38 (rust) reserved for ~10% visual weight (primary CTA, hero-eyebrow tick, siderail active state, footer dot, ASCII-flow highlight). Accent text uses `oklch(42% 0.195 38)` for 4.5:1 AA on light bg.
- **Typography:** Supreme (display, 500/600/700) + Switzer (body, 400/500/600/700) from Fontshare; mono stays ui-monospace. Neither is on the `reflex_fonts_to_reject` list. Do not swap these.
- **Spatial rhythm:** 4-px semantic scale (`--space-1..6`), `--page-max: 1120px`, fluid section padding via `clamp(64px, 3vw + 40px, 112px)`. Headings left-aligned, never centered.
- **Signature devices:** numbered section markers (`§01 · Hero` / `Fig. 01 —`) with rules through them; mono micro-text for labels; ASCII pipeline diagram in §06; fixed-left SideRail nav ≥1280px; hero grid as masked background pattern; numbered steps with monospace keys and single hairline dividers.
- **Motion:** `--motion-fast 160ms` / `--motion-base 320ms` with `cubic-bezier(0.2, 0.7, 0.3, 1)`. Scroll-reveal on AhaSection panels, grid-template-rows accordion on objections, zero bounce/elastic. Full `prefers-reduced-motion` bypass.
- **Accessibility floor:** Lighthouse 95+ on all four axes, focus-visible 2px indigo ring, skip-link, AhaReveal intersection observer with reduced-motion fallback. Non-negotiable.

### Anti-references

The landing must NOT look like:
- **Lovable / Bolt / Firebase Studio / Vercel landing clones** — cyan-on-dark, glassmorphism, purple-blue gradients, "build an app in seconds" hero photos. rntme is competing on the opposite axis ("service #10 looks like service #1").
- **Retool / Appsmith marketing** — dashboard screenshots with fake metrics, sparkline decoration, icon-grid feature sections.
- **Generic Tailwind startup kits** — centered hero, three identical icon cards, gradient text, "loved by teams at …" logo wall.
- **The reflex AI-slop palette** — dark bg + neon accents + Inter + purple-to-blue gradient + glowing buttons.

Positioning in the market framing is "safe runtime for AI-generated workflow apps" — the landing's restraint is itself the proof.

### Design Principles

1. **Restraint earns trust.** Every decorative element must carry meaning. Section markers number the argument; the ASCII flow shows the architecture; the hero grid says "this is a map, not a billboard." No pure decoration.
2. **Architecture before value.** ICP A reads architecture to decide. §03 (see-it-compile) precedes §02 (values) on purpose. Keep that ordering — do not let a future "conversion optimization" pass flip it.
3. **One accent, rarely.** Rust (OKLCH 55% 0.19 38) appears on primary CTA, the hero tick, footer dot, active siderail marker, and the ASCII-flow `<i>` highlight. That's the whole budget. Adding a second accent kills the first.
4. **Editorial typography over iconography.** Hierarchy comes from display face + size contrast + mono labels, not from icons. The landing has zero decorative icons by design.
5. **Write for service #5, not service #1.** Copy and visuals both assume the reader already shipped their first AI-built app and is now embarrassed about service #4. Do not pitch first-run wow.
6. **No database names on the page.** Storage is "durable storage" and "the schema and migration plan your service needs" — even though the token file and spec both reference the Turso/SQLite reality.
